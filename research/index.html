Brief Abstract

AI-aided Multipath Classification of Correlation Output from Software-Defined Radio GNSS Receiver

The Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) is recognized as a prevalent method for determining user position. However, positioning results can be substantially biased in urban canyons, where the presence of civil constructions can result in multipath effects and affect positioning accuracy.

Identifying multipath effects from the GNSS signal lays the solid foundation for further optimization. Knowing that multipath can cause destructive effects on the correlation process, the correlation outputs are extracted from the GNSS signal generated by the Software-Defined Radio (SDR) receivers in Hong Kong using an open-source toolbox . The correlation outputs are then manually labeled for algorithm training purposes. To avoid suboptimal performance resulting from the limitations of human intervention, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are introduced as a replacement for handcrafted algorithms to classify multipath phenomena from correlation output.

The primary objective of this research is to efficiently classify the multipath phenomenon from correlation output. The feasibility of the trained CNNs algorithm is evaluated by comparing their performance in multipath classification from the collected GNSS signal with the process of conventional methods. It is expected that the adequately trained deep learning algorithm will exhibit optimized accuracy and robustness in the identification of multipath effects from the correlation output.
